About Me

Hi there! I'm Pawan Kumar Thapaliya, a PhD candidate in Applied Physics at the University of South Florida. My research sits at the intersection of computational biophysics and neurobiology, where I use mathematical modeling, machine learning, and signal processing to explore how brain cells, particularly astrocytes, communicate and respond during stressful conditions like ischemia and epilepsy.

Astrocytes are the most abundant cell type in the brain and play a crucial role in maintaining brain function under both normal and stressful conditions. These cells not only support neurons metabolically but also help regulate ion homeostasis and neurotransmitter levels. In my research, I focus on the dynamic interactions between astrocytes and neurons, using Markov models to simulate ion dynamics (sodium, calcium) and glutamate cycling during energy deprivation. I investigate how astrocytes adapt to metabolic stress and how these adaptations impact brain health, particularly in disorders like stroke, epilepsy, and Alzheimer's disease.

My work is driven by the goal of integrating computational modeling with experimental data to uncover the mechanisms behind neurovascular coupling, synaptic function, and neuroplasticity under metabolic stress. By combining data-driven techniques like deep learning and traditional biophysical models, I aim to contribute to the development of therapeutic strategies for neurological disorders.

When I'm not immersed in research or coding, I enjoy hiking, cooking, landscape photography, and reading books on science and history.

Current Research

I am a PhD candidate in Applied Physics at the University of South Florida, conducting research in Dr. Ullah's Biophysics Lab. My work focuses on developing biophysical models to explore the intricate dynamics of astrocytes under metabolic stress conditions, particularly during chemical ischemia.

Using Markov models, I simulate ion dynamics—such as sodium and calcium signaling—to understand astrocytic responses during energy deprivation. This research aims to uncover the mechanisms of astrocyte-neuron interactions and their implications in neurological disorders like stroke, epilepsy, and Alzheimer's disease.

By combining computational modeling with experimental insights, my goal is to contribute to the development of therapeutic strategies for neurodegenerative conditions.
