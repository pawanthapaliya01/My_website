About Me

Hi, I'm Pawan Thapaliya, a PhD candidate in Applied Physics with a specialization in Computational Neuroscience. My research focuses on understanding the complex interactions between neuronal and non-neuronal cells under metabolic stress conditions, particularly in the context of ischemic events, epilepsy, and neurodegenerative diseases. I aim to bridge the gap between theoretical neuroscience and translational applications in healthcare.

Research Interests

  • Computational modeling of astrocyte function during ischemia
  • Neuroinformatics and brain-computer interfaces
  • Machine learning applications in EEG signal analysis
  • Translational research in stroke, epilepsy, and Alzheimer's disease

Current Research

At Dr. Ullah's Biophysics Lab, I'm developing detailed Markov models to simulate ion dynamics in astrocytes, focusing on sodium and calcium signaling during chemical ischemia. My research explores how NBCe1 transporters regulate pH during energy deprivation, with implications for therapeutic strategies targeting neurological disorders.

Recent Projects:

  • Biophysical modeling of NMDA and AMPA receptor activation
  • Analysis of astrocytic sodium dynamics using single-cell simulations
  • Predictive modeling of ATP consumption in neocortical vs. hippocampal astrocytes
